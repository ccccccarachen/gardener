# Plant Care Reference

Care schedules for common houseplants. Adjust watering frequency based on season:
- **Spring/Summer**: Use the shorter interval
- **Fall/Winter**: Use the longer interval (or even less frequent)

## Tropical Foliage

### Monstera Deliciosa (Swiss Cheese Plant)
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days (let top 2" dry)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Loves humidity. Yellow leaves = overwatering. Brown tips = low humidity.

### Pothos (Epipremnum aureum)
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days (let soil dry out)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Low to bright indirect
- **Notes**: Very forgiving. Wilts dramatically when thirsty but recovers fast.

### Philodendron (various)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Medium to bright indirect
- **Notes**: Similar to pothos. Heartleaf is most common.

### Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days (keep evenly moist)
- **Fertilize**: Every 6 weeks in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Low to medium indirect
- **Notes**: Droops dramatically when thirsty. Toxic to pets.

### Rubber Plant (Ficus elastica)
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Wipe leaves to remove dust. Can grow very tall.

### Fiddle Leaf Fig (Ficus lyrata)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Bright indirect (needs lots of light)
- **Notes**: Dramatic. Hates change. Brown spots = overwatering or cold drafts.

### Bird of Paradise (Strelitzia)
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days
- **Fertilize**: Every 2 weeks in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Bright direct to indirect
- **Notes**: Needs lots of light to flower indoors (rare).

### Calathea / Prayer Plant (Marantaceae family)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days (keep moist, not soggy)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Medium indirect (no direct sun)
- **Notes**: Diva plant. Needs high humidity. Use filtered water. Leaves fold at night.

### Alocasia (Elephant Ear)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days
- **Fertilize**: Every 2 weeks in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Goes dormant in winter (may lose leaves). High humidity.

## Succulents & Cacti

### Snake Plant (Sansevieria / Dracaena trifasciata)
- **Water**: Every 14-30 days (very drought tolerant)
- **Fertilize**: 2-3 times per year
- **Repot**: Every 2-3 years
- **Light**: Low to bright indirect
- **Notes**: Nearly indestructible. Great for beginners. Air purifying.

### ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia)
- **Water**: Every 14-21 days
- **Fertilize**: 2-3 times per year
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Low to bright indirect
- **Notes**: Extremely drought tolerant. Toxic if ingested.

### Aloe Vera
- **Water**: Every 14-21 days
- **Fertilize**: 2-3 times per year
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Bright indirect to direct
- **Notes**: Gel inside is medicinal. Don't overwater.

### Jade Plant (Crassula ovata)
- **Water**: Every 14-21 days
- **Fertilize**: Every 3-4 months
- **Repot**: Every 2-3 years
- **Light**: Bright direct to indirect
- **Notes**: Can live for decades. Leaves store water.

### Echeveria
- **Water**: Every 14-21 days (soak and dry method)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer (diluted)
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Bright direct
- **Notes**: Rosette shape. Stretches (etiolates) without enough light.

### String of Pearls (Senecio rowleyanus)
- **Water**: Every 14-21 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer (diluted)
- **Repot**: Rarely (shallow roots)
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Overwatering is the #1 killer. Let dry completely.

### Cactus (various)
- **Water**: Every 21-30 days (less in winter)
- **Fertilize**: 2-3 times per year
- **Repot**: Every 2-3 years
- **Light**: Bright direct
- **Notes**: Water even less in winter. Some can go months without water.

## Ferns

### Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata)
- **Water**: Every 3-7 days (keep consistently moist)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Medium indirect
- **Notes**: Needs high humidity. Mist regularly. Messy (sheds fronds).

### Maidenhair Fern (Adiantum)
- **Water**: Every 2-4 days (never let dry out)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer (diluted)
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Medium indirect (no direct sun)
- **Notes**: Very finicky. Needs constant moisture and humidity.

### Bird's Nest Fern (Asplenium nidus)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Medium indirect
- **Notes**: Easier than other ferns. Don't water into the center rosette.

## Palms & Dracaenas

### Majesty Palm (Ravenea rivularis)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days (keep moist)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Needs humidity. Brown tips are common. Spider mite prone.

### Parlor Palm (Chamaedorea elegans)
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 2-3 years
- **Light**: Low to medium indirect
- **Notes**: Slow growing. Good for low light.

### Dracaena (Corn Plant, Dragon Tree, etc.)
- **Water**: Every 10-14 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Low to bright indirect
- **Notes**: Sensitive to fluoride in tap water. Brown tips common.

### Yucca
- **Water**: Every 10-14 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Bright direct to indirect
- **Notes**: Very drought tolerant. Can handle direct sun.

## Flowering Plants

### Orchid (Phalaenopsis)
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days (let roots dry between)
- **Fertilize**: Every 2 weeks with orchid fertilizer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years (after blooming)
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Ice cube method is a myth. Soak roots instead.

### African Violet (Saintpaulia)
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days (bottom water only)
- **Fertilize**: Every 2 weeks
- **Repot**: Every 6-12 months
- **Light**: Medium indirect
- **Notes**: Never wet the leaves. Use room-temp water.

### Anthurium
- **Water**: Every 7-10 days
- **Fertilize**: Every 2 weeks in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 2 years
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Blooms are actually modified leaves (spathes).

### Bromeliad
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days (keep center cup filled)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly (mist, don't pour)
- **Repot**: Rarely (after pups form)
- **Light**: Bright indirect
- **Notes**: Mother plant dies after flowering but produces pups.

## Herbs (Indoor)

### Basil
- **Water**: Every 2-3 days (keep moist)
- **Fertilize**: Every 2 weeks
- **Repot**: As needed
- **Light**: Bright direct (6+ hours)
- **Notes**: Pinch flowers to prolong harvest. Short-lived.

### Mint
- **Water**: Every 2-3 days
- **Fertilize**: Monthly
- **Repot**: Frequently (spreads aggressively)
- **Light**: Bright indirect to direct
- **Notes**: Very invasive. Keep contained.

### Rosemary
- **Water**: Every 7-14 days (let dry out)
- **Fertilize**: Monthly in spring/summer
- **Repot**: Every 1-2 years
- **Light**: Bright direct (6+ hours)
- **Notes**: Prefers drier conditions. Good drainage essential.

## General Rules of Thumb

### Watering
- Stick finger 2" into soil — if dry, water
- Most plants prefer to dry out slightly between waterings
- Overwatering kills more plants than underwatering
- Water less in winter (plants are dormant)
- Use room-temperature water

### Fertilizing
- Only fertilize during active growth (spring/summer)
- Stop fertilizing in fall/winter
- Less is more — dilute to half strength if unsure
- Never fertilize a dry plant (water first)

### Repotting
- Best time: early spring
- Only go up 1-2" in pot size
- Signs you need to repot: roots coming out of drainage holes, water runs straight through, plant is top-heavy
- Don't repot a stressed or sick plant

### Light
- "Bright indirect" = near a window but no direct sun rays
- "Low light" = survives but may not thrive
- "Direct sun" = actual sunbeams hitting the plant
- Most tropicals burn in direct sun

### Humidity
- Most tropical plants like 50%+ humidity
- Group plants together to increase humidity
- Pebble trays and humidifiers help
- Misting is mostly ineffective (but won't hurt)
